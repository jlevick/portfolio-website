The Challenge

Design the minimum viable product (MVP) of a mobile app that allows users to instantly connect with astrologers in order to ask astro-related questions.

What is Aspect?

Aspect is a comprehensive astrology resource offering access to your unique birth chart, vetted professional astrologers, and a community of astro-enthusiasts.

01 Research

Astrology is inherently personal, offering meaning and guidance in increasingly despairing times. With this in mind, it is no wonder that the “mystical services” industry is worth $2.2 billion and growing, with venture capitalists backing astrology apps like Aspect. Right now there are no other apps that provide what Aspect does, allowing us prime entry in to a budding and profitable market.

Top 3 Market Gaps to Fill

(Based on S.W.O.T analyses of Sanctuary Astrology and Kasamba).

  • Pricing - Upfront, Transparent pricing model.
  • Qualifications - Vetted astrologers with qualifications listed.
  • Reviews - Easy to find user reviews.

After determining the viability of the project, I surveyed 22 people in order to learn who they are, how invested they are in astrology, and what they look for in an astrologer. The survey was open to all.

The most important criteria when seeking an astrologer:

  • Verified Expertise (63.6%)
  • Astrologer's Background (63.6%)
  • Client Reviews (45.5%)

People ultimately don't book astrology readings because they are:

  • Unsure of the astrologer's credibility (64.7%)
  • Unsure of whom to book with (64.7%)

The survey was followed up by user interviews with four self identified astro-enthusiasts. I wanted to better understand why people are interested in astrology, why they do (or don't) work with an astrologer, and what they expect from astrological content.

Key Findings

  • Users engage with astrology for fun, connection, and self knowledge.
  • Users won't book readings if they are not certain the astrologer is trustworthy.
  • No single astrology resource provides all the information users want.

The interviews revealed users are looking for more than just access to vetted astrologers.
Based on these findings I crafted a problem statement and initial hypothesis:

Users need a convenient way to discover qualified astrologers, connect with fellow astrology enthusiasts, and learn about themselves through their own unique birth chart.

We will know our problem statement to be true when users can connect with other astrology lovers and no longer waste time searching for an astrologer or researching their personal astrology - all in one single app.

If we focus on:

  • Offering inclusive astrology experts
  • Matching users with astrologers
  • Showing costs and reviews up front
  • Providing personalized astrology content
  • Creating an interactive community space
We will be well positioned to break in to the market with a product like no other.

02 Ideation

User Persona's

Thanks to the prior research and hours of affinity mapping, two distinct personas were created. User flows helped me plan what features and screens were necessary in order for a user to complete a given task.

The culmination resulted in Three Main App Features:

  • A way for Hannah to find an astrologer who fits their needs (Browse and Chat).
  • A way for Vanessa to connect with others (Community Chat).
  • A way for Hannah to delve in to self exploration (My Chart).

Once I knew who I was designing for and what screens I absolutely needed, I employed a hybrid card sort to form the site map for Aspect. I then began lo-fi wireframing with pen & paper. In order to bring clarity to my designs I chose to include copy. I began designing in Figma once I felt confident in my direction.

04 Testing

Now that I had designed three distinct features I felt would meet the expectations of our persona's, Vanessa and Hannah, I needed to put them to the test.
The goal of usability testing was to measure for usability errors with the goal of ensuring an easy, intuitive user experience.

Example of one task scenario:
"You've registered for Aspect and can now see if there are any astrologers you might be interested in connecting with. How would you go about finding someone and then connecting with them? Please show me."

Moderated remote testing was used for all 6 usability tests. Participants were given four tasks to complete. This link opens a new window to test results.

Key Findings:

  • Information Overload:
    Astrologer's profile is too busy.
  • Confusing Copy:
    “Readings” copy on menu is confusing.
  • Expectations:
    Users expected a way to contact astrologer prior to booking.

Changes Implemented:

  • Designed drop downs for About Me and Qualifications in astrologer's profile.
  • Changed copy from “Readings” (in menu) to “Readers”.
  • Designed Messages feature (and button/copy on astrologer's profile).

Preference Testing

The feedback I received from usability testing allowed me reduce confusion by cleaning up screens.
Based on this testing I redesigned three screens and 12 participants voted on which they liked best.

06 Conclusion

As a new product on the market, the KPI (key performance indicator) I would measure is user return rate. This is a relatively easy metric to track and holds invaluable insight. How many users are booking an astrologer through our platform, and how many are returning to book again? If the user experience is poor because of the apps' flow, the user interface, the copy/content, the astrology experts, or any other number of reasons, we may find users do not return.

Designs took in to account web accessibility guidelines, usability heuristics, and the real-life feedback of everyone who helped shape Aspect.

I designed a minimum viable product with 3 distinct features that offers users exactly what they need, all in one convenient app.

I believe Aspect is a genuinely profitable business venture. I would like to acknowledge the pool from which I tested was extremely small and therefore all future testing would need to be done on a much larger scale with more participants. With this said, I stand behind the idea of Aspect as an entry point in to the growing market of Mystical Services.
